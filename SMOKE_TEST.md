## 3. `/brain-reset` — Open Loops Dump branch

> **User:** /brain-reset
>
> **Skill:** Which one is this?
> A) **Overwhelmed** — my head is full of stuff I'm worried about and I can't think.
> B) **Under-stimulated** — I'm bored, drifting, scrolling, can't engage.
>
> **User:** A
>
> **Skill:** Okay. Dump everything. Tasks, worries, half-thoughts, the email you're dreading, the thing you forgot to text someone. One per line. No order. Just empty your head into the next message.
>
> **User:**
> - dentist appointment I haven't booked
> - mom's birthday next weekend
> - that bug from work still in my head
> - need to do laundry
> - keep forgetting to refill my prescription
> - what if the landlord doesn't renew the lease
> - haven't replied to Jamie's text from Tuesday
> - kitchen sink is slow
>
> **Skill:**
>
> **Now**
> - Refill prescription → Next step: open your pharmacy's app and tap "refill" on the current bottle.
> - Reply to Jamie → Next step: send one sentence — "Hey, sorry for the delay, was underwater. Want to catch up this week?"
> - Mom's birthday → Next step: put it on your calendar with a reminder for two days before, then text one sibling to coordinate.
>
> **Later**
> - Book dentist appointment (this week, not today)
> - Laundry (tonight or tomorrow)
> - Kitchen sink (sometime — note it, don't fix it now)
>
> **Trash**
> - "What if the landlord doesn't renew the lease" — worry-shaped, no action attached. If/when it happens, you'll deal with it. Today it's not yours.
> - "That bug from work still in my head" — work hours are over. The bug doesn't care that you're thinking about it.
>
> Pick one Now item. That's the only thing that exists for the next 20 minutes.
