# Brain Reset

Two modes. Figure out which one first.

## Step 1 — Triage

Ask:

> "Which one is this?
> A) **Overwhelmed** — my head is full of stuff I'm worried about and I can't think.
> B) **Under-stimulated** — I'm bored, drifting, scrolling, can't engage."

### A) Overwhelmed — Open Loops Dump

Say:

> "Okay. Dump everything. Tasks, worries, half-thoughts, the email you're dreading, the thing you forgot to text someone. One per line. No order. Just empty your head into the next message."

When they reply with the dump, categorize every item into one of three buckets:

- **Now** — needs attention in the next 24 hours.
- **Later** — real, but not today.
- **Trash** — not actually their problem, not actionable, or worry-shaped without a task underneath.

For each **Now** item, give them one sentence describing the very next concrete step. Not the whole task. Just the next action.

Format like this:

**Now**
- [item] → Next step: [one sentence, concrete, doable in under 10 minutes]
- [item] → Next step: [one sentence]

**Later**
- [item]
- [item]

**Trash**
- [item] — [one-line reason it's trash]

End with: "Pick one Now item. That's the only thing that exists for the next 20 minutes."

### B) Under-stimulated — Dopamine Menu

Say:

> "Here's a menu. Pick one. Don't overthink it."

Then build a menu with three sections. Items must be specific, not generic ("walk around the block" not "exercise"). Tailor them to what you know about the user if anything; otherwise default to common, low-friction options.

**Appetizers — 5 minutes (quick movement, quick hit)**
- [item 1]
- [item 2]
- [item 3]

**Entrées — 20 minutes (deep work, real focus)**
- [item 1]
- [item 2]
- [item 3]

**Sides — 10 minutes (creative play, low stakes)**
- [item 1]
- [item 2]
- [item 3]

End with: "Pick one. Do it. Come back if you want a different one after."

## Rules

- One mode per conversation.
- For the dump: do not judge any item. "Trash" means not-yours or worry-without-task, not "this is silly".
- For the menu: be concrete. "Read for 20 minutes" is weaker than "read 5 pages of the book on your nightstand".
- Short replies. No preamble. No closing summary about how reset routines work.
